Our location

We will be located locally, in Plaquemine. Our only requirements for a location are proximity to residence, good internet, and availability of performance computer parts. Because of the internet, anyone can buy anything from anywhere; and this allows us to be located anywhere. We do not need performant internet because our business wouldn't perceive any benefit and it would be an unnecessary expense. Therefore, we could operate our business (almost) anywhere with a good internet infastructure. Which leaves us to one final requirement: proximity to residence. We have options for decent internet in Plaquemine, and we live in close proximity, so it would be most reasonable to operate locally.

Us VS. Comparable Companies

Apple

Apple is an interesting point of comparison. Apple is both a software and hardware company, and they do both really well. However, Apple has a very different philosophy for running a business and aesthetics. Apple doesn't run a business with their own self interest as first priority, but that doesn't mean they don't care about the consumer. Apple prioritizes the consumer second. Historically, Apple has always been highly oppressive about their policies and restrictions for their platforms. For example, Apple is very strict about what you can and cannot do with their products. Apple makes it very hard and expensive for developers to create for their platforms, like the iPhone. Apple also charges excessive amounts of money, for their hardware. For example, the CHEAPEST new MacBook available to purchase is over $1,000 now and the hardware is only worth about $500. Apple also charges over $300 for a $7 RAM chip, and $400 for $40 flash storage chip in a MacBook. We will create both software and hardware that is accessible, cheap, and easy for third party developers. We also prioritize the consumer over our own company. For example, if we release an office product and a customer has a problem with it, we will make sure that it gets resolved so the consumer is happy, no matter how much it may cost the company. If we release a mobile messaging app and a customer wants to be able to cusomize the interface beyond just light and dark mode, we will work our hardest to accomplish it. If the customer sees a point of improvement to be made in our products, we will improve it. Our products will also be open for people to do what they want with them (within reason).

Microsoft

This one is quick. While Microsoft is open about how you use their hardware and software, they are an anti-consumer, monopolistic, and anti-competitive company. They don't make very good hardware, and their software is very flawed and buggy. Microsoft, much like Apple, regularly charges over $1,000 for $300 computers. We actually care about the consumer and treat our competition fairly. We also make sure that all software is complete, bugs are patched, and flaws are fixed before release. We will not release hardware unless it is good and worth while for the customer.

Nintendo

While Nintendo doesn't make computers, they still do make both hardware and software very well. Their games are enjoyable and replayable. Their hardware is unique and creative. Nintendo is also (mostly) accessible to developers on their platforms. However, just like Microsoft, Nintendo is an anti-consumer company. Nintendo frequently abuses online content reporting and DMCA systems. For example, Nintendo goes after small content creators with copyright strikes claiming they are using Nintendo's music for purposes unintended by Nintendo, while they do not go after larger content creators who do the same thing. Additionally, just like Apple, Nintendo is very oppressive and strict about what you do with their products. We will be very open with our hardware and software, and we will allow assets like music in our video games to be used freely without restrictions.
