Company Salaries (Apr 6, 2023)

An alternative way of setting up a company's employee (and CEO) salary: Salaries would be based purely on risk factor. There would be 4 different grades for risk factor; no risk, minor injuries may occur, possibility of being hospitalized, and risk of death. However, since the CEO is the brains of a company, he can only be payed as much as the company's riskiest job. The company divides up the entire company revenue into 2 categories, company needs, and salaries. The company can ONLY spend their company needs money on necessities to run, e.g. supplies, tax, and utility bills. Neither the CEO nor employees can use the company needs money for their own benefit. The company needs money is subtracted from the total revenue, then it is divided amongst the employees (and CEO) based on the job risk factor.

Goals are depressing (Apr 21-Jul 22, 2023 This one took me a while to properly put into words)

While many may oppose, I believe that goals are depressing. Sure goals can give you something to look forward to, push you to be better, get through the day, or simply keep you busy, but if you fail to accomplish your goals, you could do more harm than the good caused by achieving it. If you simply miss 2 or 3 goals in a row, you would significantly decrease morale, no matter how enthusiastic or upbeat you may be.




Products per month or quality per product? (Jul 19, 2023)

Background knowledge:

At the time of writing this, Retroid is a handheld Android gaming hardware manufacturer that releases new consoles sometimes several times a month and so frequently that people are starting to switch to other brands because of buyers remorse due to the company making the hardware obsolete before the consumer can be satisfied with their purchase.

Soliloquy:

Retroid's idea might be that the more systems they release and the more frequent they release them might mean more customers in their eyes because they have more options to appease a customer that might not have bought a retroid otherwise. However, I do agree that the releasing these consoles this frequently will give their customers a sour tsste because they associated Retroid with the regret of buying one and something better coming out right after. So i can see where they are coming from, but I agree that they should slow down a bit and take more time to optimize software and hardware before releasing a new hardware. If they take the same approach that phone manufacturers take by releasing maybe 3 phones a year around the same time, one low end, one mid range, and one high end, they probably would strike an even balence.




Just because you bought a digital game or movie, doesn't mean you own it. (Jul 21, 2023)

If I don't own anything, I won't buy anything. The gaming industry believes if you buy a digital game, you don't own it, you're renting it. It is wrong for a man to sell someone a house and take it back when he wants more money, but it is perfectly fine if a company sells someone a game and revokes access after a few years because they want you to buy the remaster or the sequel. For example, if you bought Trackmania from the Microsoft store, then a day later it is removed from the Microsoft store, you lose access to the game and it is automatically uninstalled from your device. HOW IS THIS LEGAL‽ Microsoft and Ubisoft and EA and many others seem to thing that pressing the "buy" button on a digital game doesn't mean that you OWN the game, but you are RENTING the game at full price. As of this very moment, they do not mention this anywhere in their terms of service nor when you "purchase" the game. If you are renting the game, then why does it say "buy" on the button and "purchased" when the transaction goes through? Why doesn't it say "rent" and "enjoy while you can?"HOW IS THIS LEGAL‽ I don't understand why this type of thing isn't talked about yet. The gaming industry isn't even the only one doing this, Amazon is doing it too with their video streaming service.
